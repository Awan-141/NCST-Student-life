Atomic Structure

  • Protons: Positively charged particles in the nucleus
  • Neutrons: Neutral particles in the nucleus
  • Electrons: Negatively charged particles orbiting the nucleus

Mole and Relative Atomic Mass (RAM)

Mole: A mole is a unit used to count particles (atoms, molecules, or ions) in chemistry. It is defined as the amount of substance containing the same number of particles as there are atoms in 12 grams of carbon-12. This number is called Avogadro's Number, which is \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\).

Relative Atomic Mass (RAM): The RAM of an element is the weighted average mass of all its isotopes, measured relative to 1/12th the mass of a carbon-12 atom.

Formula:

\(\text{RAM} = \frac{\Sigma (\text{isotopic mass} \times \text{abundance})}{100}\)

Example: Chlorine has two isotopes: Cl-35 (75%) and Cl-37 (25%).

\(\text{RAM} = (35 \times 0.75) + (37 \times 0.25) = 35.5\)

The average relative atomic mass of chlorine is **35.5**.

Avogadro's Number

Avogadro's Number: Avogadro's number (\(6.022 \times 10^{23}\)) defines the number of particles in one mole of a substance.

For example, 1 mole of water (\(H_2O\)) contains \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) molecules of water.

Molar Gas Volume

Molar Gas Volume: At standard temperature and pressure (STP), one mole of any gas occupies 22.4 liters.

This means that 1 mole of oxygen gas (\(O_2\)) or nitrogen gas (\(N_2\)) will occupy the same volume of 22.4 L under STP conditions.

Empirical and Molecular Formulas

Empirical formulas represent the simplest whole-number ratio of elements in a compound. Molecular formulas give the actual number of each type of atom in a molecule.

Percent Composition

Percent composition represents the percentage by mass of each element in a compound.

Chemical Reactions and Energy Changes

Chemical reactions involve breaking bonds in reactants and forming new bonds in products. Energy is absorbed or released during these processes.

Reversible Reactions and Equilibrium

Reversible reactions can proceed in both forward and reverse directions. At equilibrium, the rates of the forward and reverse reactions are equal.

Example: The reaction between nitrogen dioxide and dinitrogen tetroxide:

\(2NO_2 \leftrightarrow N_2O_4\)
