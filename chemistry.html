Atoms and the Periodic Table

The periodic table organizes elements based on their atomic number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties.

Mole and Relative Atomic Mass (RAM)

The mole is a unit used in chemistry to measure the amount of substance. Relative Atomic Mass (RAM) is the weighted average mass of an element's atoms.

Mole Concept

The mole concept is fundamental to understanding chemical reactions and simplifying calculations involving chemical amounts.

Molar Gas Volume, Gas Volumes, and Solution Concentrations

Molar gas volume refers to the volume occupied by one mole of an ideal gas at standard temperature and pressure (STP), typically 22.4 L.

Empirical and Molecular Formulae

Empirical formula represents the simplest whole-number ratio of elements in a compound, while the molecular formula gives the exact number of atoms of each element in a molecule.

Percent Composition of Elements in a Compound

Percent composition refers to the percentage by mass of each element in a compound.

Chemical Reactions and Energy Changes

Chemical reactions involve the rearrangement of atoms to form new substances. These reactions can be exothermic or endothermic.

Reversible Chemical Reactions and Equilibrium

In reversible reactions, the products can react to form the original reactants, leading to a dynamic equilibrium.

Effect of Conditions on Reversible Reactions

Le Chatelier's principle states that if a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it will shift to counteract the change and restore equilibrium.

Acid-Carbonate Reactions and Carbon Dioxide Production

Acid-carbonate reactions involve the reaction of an acid with a carbonate to produce carbon dioxide, water, and a salt.
