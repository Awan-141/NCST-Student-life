Atomic Structure

  • Protons: Positively charged particles in the nucleus
  • Neutrons: Neutral particles in the nucleus
  • Electrons: Negatively charged particles orbiting the nucleus

Mole and Relative Atomic Mass (RAM)

RAM = (Σ (isotopic mass × abundance)) / 100

Example: Chlorine has two isotopes, Cl-35 (75%) and Cl-37 (25%)

RAM = (35 × 0.75) + (37 × 0.25) = 35.5

Avogadro's Number

Avogadro's number (6.022 × 10²³) defines the number of particles in one mole of a substance.

Molar Gas Volume

At standard temperature and pressure (STP), one mole of any gas occupies 22.4 L.
