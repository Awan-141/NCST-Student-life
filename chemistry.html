Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table

Atoms are the building blocks of matter. They consist of protons, neutrons, and electrons. The periodic table organizes elements based on their atomic structure.

Bonding and Compound Formation

Chemical bonding involves the sharing or transfer of electrons between atoms. The main types are ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding.

Moles and Relative Mass

The mole is a unit used to express amounts of particles in chemistry. It's related to Avogadro's number and helps us work with very large numbers of atoms or molecules.
